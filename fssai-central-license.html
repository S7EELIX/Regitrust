Who Should Consider Central License

  • Food importers and export-linked food businesses
  • Multi-state food operators
  • Large manufacturers, processors, and storage operators
  • E-commerce food businesses or marketplace-linked food operators
  • Businesses that exceed state license limits or fall under central category

State, Central, or Renewal?

If the business is mainly within one state and does not fall into a central category, review FSSAI State License. If an existing license is near expiry, use FSSAI License Renewal.

Documents Required

  • Business constitution and PAN details
  • Premises proof and layout documents
  • Product category and food activity details
  • Import/export or IEC details, where applicable
  • Authorized signatory and technical person details, where required
  • Food safety management documents and declarations, where applicable

Process

  1. Confirm whether central license is required
  2. Review products, premises, activity, and turnover

  4. Prepare and file FoSCoS application
  5. Track query, inspection, or authority response

Frequently Asked Questions

Who needs FSSAI Central License?

Eligibility depends on activity, turnover, scale, import/export involvement, e-commerce operations, multi-state activity, and specific food category rules.

Can a business upgrade from State to Central License?

Yes, if the business scale or category changes. The right filing path depends on current license status and business facts.

Is the starts-from fee final?

No. Final quote depends on activity, premises, product category, government fee, inspection, and query scope.
