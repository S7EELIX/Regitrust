One Person Company (OPC) Registration

OPC registration helps a single founder start a company with limited liability, separate legal identity, nominee structure, and corporate credibility. Regitrust supports DSC, name approval, incorporation documents, nominee consent, SPICe+ filing, PAN/TAN, and post-registration compliance guidance.

Best For

Solo founders, consultants, freelancers, service providers, and small businesses that want company status without co-founders.

Typical Timeline

Usually 7-12 working days after complete documents, name approval, and MCA processing conditions are clear.

Who Should Choose OPC

  • Solo founders starting independently
  • Consultants, freelancers, or service providers needing corporate status
  • Individuals wanting limited liability protection
  • Small businesses that may later convert into private limited company
  • Entrepreneurs who do not want partnership ownership

Documents Required

  • PAN and Aadhaar of member and nominee
  • Address proof of member and nominee
  • Photographs and contact details
  • Registered office address proof and NOC
  • Consent of nominee in prescribed form
  • Proposed company names and business activity details

Process

  1. Check eligibility, nominee, and business activity
  2. Obtain DSC for the proposed director
  3. Apply for company name approval
  4. Prepare MOA, AOA, nominee consent, and incorporation documents
  5. File SPICe+ forms with MCA
  6. Receive Certificate of Incorporation, PAN, TAN, and next-step guidance

After OPC Registration

  • Maintain books of accounts
  • Open current bank account
  • Plan GST registration if applicable
  • File annual ROC forms and income tax return
  • Track conversion requirement if statutory limits or business plans change

Frequently Asked Questions

Can an OPC have more than one shareholder?

No. OPC can have only one member, but it must have one nominee.

Is audit mandatory for OPC?

OPCs generally require statutory audit under company law, even though ownership is held by one member.

Is OPC better than proprietorship?

OPC gives separate legal identity and limited liability, but it also has company-law compliance. The right choice depends on business risk, scale, and compliance comfort.
