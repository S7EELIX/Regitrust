Who Needs Annual ROC Filing

  • Private limited companies completing yearly compliance
  • One Person Companies with annual filing obligations
  • Companies with no business activity but active status
  • Businesses needing clean MCA master data
  • Companies preparing for loans, tenders, funding, or due diligence

Documents Required

  • Audited financial statements
  • Board report and AGM details, where applicable
  • Director and shareholder details
  • DSC of authorised director
  • Previous filing records and company master data
  • Auditor and statutory information

Process

  1. Review company status, due dates, and pending filings
  2. Collect financial statements and statutory information
  3. Prepare applicable forms such as AOC-4 and MGT-7 or MGT-7A
  4. File forms with MCA using DSC and required certifications
  5. Download challans and maintain filing acknowledgements
  6. Share next annual compliance calendar

Common Mistakes

  • Assuming no turnover means no ROC filing
  • Confusing income tax return with ROC filing
  • Filing without matching financial and statutory records
  • Missing AGM, audit, or board documentation requirements
  • Waiting until additional fees increase the cost

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ROC filing required every year?

Yes. Active companies must complete annual ROC filing every financial year.

What happens if annual filing is delayed?

Additional fees apply and long delays may create default risk for the company and directors.

Is income tax return the same as ROC filing?

No. ROC filing is under company law, while income tax return is filed separately with the Income Tax Department.
