Why ROC Filing Matters

ROC filings keep your business legally active and compliant. Missed filings can lead to penalties, director disqualification risk, reduced credibility, and difficulties with banking, funding, tender participation, due diligence, or future corporate actions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ROC filing required even if there is no business activity?

Yes. Companies and LLPs may still have annual filing obligations even when there is no revenue or business activity.

What happens if ROC filing is delayed?

Delayed filing can attract additional fees and may create compliance complications for the business and directors.

Can Regitrust help with previous-year pending filings?

Yes. Regitrust can review pending filings, estimate the scope, and help prepare a corrective filing plan.

Do LLPs and private limited companies have the same filing requirements?

No. LLPs and companies have different forms, due dates, and compliance requirements.

Is the starts-from fee the final ROC filing cost?

No. The final quote depends on entity type, pending years, delay fees, accounting readiness, audit status, forms required, and whether additional compliance work is needed.
