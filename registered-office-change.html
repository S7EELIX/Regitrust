Change Type

Typical Timeline

Same-city changes can be quicker; state or ROC jurisdiction changes may need more approvals and time.

When Registered Office Change Is Needed

  • Company has shifted to a new office address
  • Business moved from rented to owned premises
  • Startup is updating coworking or virtual office details
  • Company is changing state or ROC jurisdiction
  • MCA records show an outdated or incorrect address

What Is Included

  • Office shift type review
  • Address proof and NOC checklist
  • Board documentation support
  • MCA form preparation guidance
  • Status tracking and acknowledgement sharing
  • Post-change update checklist for GST, bank, and licenses

Documents Required

  • Board resolution
  • Address proof of new registered office
  • NOC from property owner
  • Rent agreement or ownership proof
  • Utility bill for new office
  • Company master data and DSC details
  • Additional documents for state or ROC jurisdiction change, if applicable

Process

  1. Identify whether the shift is same city, same state, different ROC, or different state
  2. Collect proof, NOC, board approval, and company records
  3. Prepare applicable MCA forms and supporting details
  4. File address change with MCA
  5. Track approval or acknowledgement
  6. Guide GST, bank, invoice, and license updates

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a residential address be registered office?

Yes, if proper address proof and owner NOC are available and the business can maintain statutory communication at that address.

Is shareholder approval always required?

It depends on the shift type. Same-city changes are usually simpler, while state-level changes can require more approvals.

Does registered office change affect GST?

GST address and other business registrations should be reviewed after the MCA address change.

Is the starts-from fee final?

No. Final pricing depends on shift type, documents, government fees, delay exposure, and linked amendment work.
